Looking at the history of Australia's active cases, we can see that there were three major outbreaks which have occured: one at the start of April 2020, one at the middle of August 2020, and our worst one yet - one in late September 2021. Severity of Symptoms

Despite COVID being highly contagious, approximately only 15% of individuals experience moderate symptoms, and only 5% experience severe symptoms. These are usually older individuals aged 70 and above alongside those who possess chronic health conditions.

From the area chart on the right, we can see that the number of people who needed to go to hospital as a result of contracting COVID coincides with the pattern of the active case numbers. Of those who do need medical attention, we can once again see that only a small proportion of these individuals suffer from severe symptoms, thus need to either be in ICU or use a ventilator.

COVID Associated Deaths

During the second outbreak in August 2020, the case fatality was approximately 4.9%. However, with the administration of COVID vaccines starting on the 22nd of February, we can see that the fatality reduced down to 0.3%, despite incurring all time high case records. These numbers likewise showcases the impact in which the vaccines are having on strengthening the body's immune system against COVID.

Days in Lockdown

On the 4th of October, Victoria made news headlines as being 'the most locked down city in the world'. Prior to the distribution of covid vaccines, premier Daniel Andrews adopted a 0-case strategy, whereby a lockdown would ensue given a spike in case numbers and only be lifted once these numbers hit 0.

Albeit this strategy worked in controlling the first two outbreaks, Mr. Andrews realised that this strategy would be impractical given the massive surge in case numbers as of the 29th of September. Concurrently, Mr.Andrews has redirected the strategy to ending Victoria's 6th lockdown given that it reaches a double dose vaccination rate of 70%.
